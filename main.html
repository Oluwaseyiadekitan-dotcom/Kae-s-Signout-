The Truth

Nobody tells you what it actually takes.

People celebrate the title. I celebrated the Tuesday nights. The ones where you had three exams coming and zero sleep and still showed up the next morning like you had something to prove.

You did have something to prove, by the way. And you proved it. Repeatedly. For eight years straight.

I am not going to pretend I understood every sacrifice you made. But I was there for it. Quietly. Cheering loudly on the inside.

Year by Year

Eight chapters of one great story.

Year 1
You walked in.
Not knowing what was coming. That took courage you probably did not realize you had yet.
Year 2
The grind began.
Anatomy. Physiology. The kind of syllabuses designed to make you question your life choices. You passed them anyway.
Year 3
You stayed.
This is the year most people wobble. You did not wobble. I watched. I noticed.
Year 4
Halfway and still going.
If the mountain looked big at year one, by year four you were basically living on it. You kept climbing.
Year 5
The wards hit different.
Real patients. Real pressure. You went from textbooks to lives and you handled it with such quiet dignity it honestly amazed me.
Year 6
The weight of it all.
Medicine does not let you be casual. You carried its weight without letting it flatten you. That is not a small thing.
Year 7
The final stretch kills people.
You found something deeper in year seven. Like someone who had decided they were going to finish this no matter what.
Year 8
Doctor.
Dr. Ebiyomare Kelvin Edesiri. Eight years and you got there. I am still not over it honestly.

Kelvin,

I have been trying to write this for weeks. Every version sounded either too formal or too much like a card from a shop. So here is the real version.

Watching you do this for eight years taught me something about the kind of person you are. You do not quit. Not when it is hard. Not even when you really probably wanted to.

You showed up. Every single time. And that is one of the most genuinely impressive things I have ever watched a person do.

The white coat looks good on you. But honestly it always looked like it was coming. Like the world was just catching up to who you already were.

I love you. I am so proud of you it is actually a bit embarrassing. And I cannot wait to see what you do next.

Yours, always The one who was cheering from the beginning
